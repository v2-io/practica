# 03-concrete prep — four perspectives to distinguish

**Captured durably so it isn't lost when 03 authoring begins.** Joseph, 2026-05-20: "I want to say this before I forget — when we get to 03 we're going to want to carefully distinguish between" four registers that are easy to conflate. Verbatim:

1. **Implications for Goal/Objective/Strategy from agent & locus/composite perspectives generally.** Theory-general — applies to any task/strategy tool built on AAT, not just practica.

2. **Implications for our initial Practica build process & patterns.** Reflexive / dogfooding — how *we* build practica using these principles. Process for the builders.

3. **Implications for known external users — initially ASF in particular, who needs task/goal tracking urgently (the impetus for starting this now).** Concrete-customer — ASF has a need today; practica's initial form must serve it.

4. **Implications for the Practica *product* design, architecture, and feature pipeline.** Product-aspirational — what the mature practica becomes.

Joseph: *"All of those eventually converge as this project/product matures — but, at least in my mind, it is easy to conflate those things still."*

## Why this matters at 03

The same harvested claim can wear all four hats. Example: *"the intent layer should support recurring re-attending"* might be:
- (1) a *general* claim about any composite agent's intent layer
- (2) a *build-process* constraint on how practica's intent layer is constructed by us
- (3) a *near-term ASF need* (or not — depends on what ASF actually asks for)
- (4) a *product feature* on the roadmap

Same content, four different valences. Conflating them either over-scopes (treating an ASF-specific need as a universal theory implication, baking it into the foundation) or under-scopes (treating a universal theory implication as merely a feature on a roadmap, missing its load-bearing reach). Convergence *across* registers is itself important information — implications that wear all four hats simultaneously are the most load-bearing and should be 03's structural anchors.

## How to apply during 03 authoring

Each implication carried forward from [[02-normative-harvest]] (and from the AAT-sweep and operata-pass and any successor passes) should be sortable into one or more of these four registers. The natural approach: *tag* each implication with the register(s) it belongs to, and write the corresponding 03 content under the right register heading. Convergence across registers becomes a visible property, not a hidden one.

This file is captured here so it's findable when 03 work begins. The four-perspective distinction should propagate into 03's structure — possibly as the top-level organization, possibly as a per-claim tagging convention, possibly both.
